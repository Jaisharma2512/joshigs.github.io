Elsewhere

Exploring possibilities for a premium media experience.

Project Summary

Elsewhere is a media app that offers free media content and wants to implement a paid subscription service to establish a profitable revenue stream.

Problem

How might we aid Elsewhere to implement a paid subscription service while retaining users who cannot afford premium services.

Solution

Design a premium media player/home interface for ElsewhereImplement a paid subscription service that appeals to both new and existing users; ensuring the conversion rate of premium users without losing users who are okay with the free core functionalityDesign their brand identity with a color palette and unique expeirence for Identify an ethical price point for the premium media service

Market Research

Competitive Analysis

A competitive analysis was performed to:
1. Synthesizing the information collected before designing the widget
2. Identify common pain points
3. Identify features that we should include in the widget
4. Enhance our features by taking notes from the competitors widgets

We looked at three categories of competitors (Independent desktop online web outfit builders, Mobile App Outfit Generators, and Retailer-Integrated Outfit Builders) and gave them a heuristic rating based on the site’s usability

Pain Points:‍

  • No clear CTA or end task. Users are not shown a clear purpose of why they're building the outfits
  • No Creative Freedom. Users are not able to compile the clothes in a way that helps them envision the outfit for themselves
  • Limited Ability. User's are only able to click one item of each category at a time, and often lose track of what they are building due to a lack of functionality.

The Solution

  • Outfit Gallery. Beautiful and usable for users, promotes sharing the creation between users and friends within the product and on social media platforms
  • Flexible User Control. Allow users to add items such as accessories, decorative layers, text, as well as a tab for past outfits created to aid inspiration.
  • Prioritize Social Sharing in an effort to create additional organic traffic, ultimately increasing the conversion of overall sales for the retailers.
  • End Goal. Likewise, having a clear end goal and an intuitive path for users to follow is imperative to create an experience that they will want to return to use time and time again.

Conclusion

Overall, this project was fun to create as I was able to work with a client to meet their needs. I've worked on an outfit builder for my first capstone project, but working on it as a white label widget was fun as it forced me to perform different market research to better conceptualize how to design it.

The most challenging part was designing for the scope of the project. As there was a lot of routes we could have taken to make it visually appealing and intuitive, we only had 4 weeks for the whole project, so we had to consolidate on only features that provided the most value to the product.
